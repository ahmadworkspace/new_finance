Helpful information on equipment loans

Read some of the helpful information on equipment loans to help you make a right decision

Buying New or Used

What’s the best for you?

Buying new is more expensive, buying used is cheaper. But is it so cut and dry? Often buying new means massive (and instant) depreciation. Buying used may only drive you so far until you need repairs and replacement parts. What is best for your situation? If you are buying new and you’re planning for a family, buying something larger may suit you well for five or even ten years. A comparable used equipment may have a much shorter lifespan.

When Zero doesn’t equal “nothing” – 0% loans

Chances are you’ve seen equipment dealers offer “zero percent” loans on TV or radio. These may sound enticing, but are not all they’re cracked up to be. Dealers offering “zero” percent loans may add hidden fees, penalties and force you to buy older models at the retail price. That might mean paying more for last year’s model. Buying a retail equipment loan from a reputable broker or lender while negotiating a better price is a better option that will save you money in the long run.

Car loan repayments and your budget

Buying a equipment with finance means monthly repayments. It makes financial sense to budget for your repayments so you never run into trouble. But does it give you the full picture of how much you’ll be paying? Budgeting for repayments is a good first step, but you must also consider consumables and other expenses. Insurance, fuel, servicing and incidentals such as cleaning may play into your bottom line.

Buying private vs dealer purchase – what’s best?

In the equipment buying world the age-old debate of buying private and buying from a dealer rages on. What is best? It depends on your objectives. If you want to save money and don’t mind the inconvenience of travel, no-shows and checking documents and VINs online, buying private is the way to go. Dealer sales are almost always more expensive, but you get warranties, protection under consumer law and after-sales support. What is more important? Price, convenience or added safety?

FAQs

1. what is a equipment loan?

A equipment loan is a personal financial product in which a lender provides a private individual with the funds necessary to purchase a equipment or similar vehicle.

2.How can I apply for a equipment loan?

You can start your application by getting a free, no-obligation quote. One of our friendly financial professionals will contact you and guide you through the process.

3. what is a secured equipment loan?

A secured equipment loan is a financial product that ties the amount of the loan to the value of the car. The equipment is used as collateral. In return for the security, your equipment loan interest rate is generally lower.

4. How much can I borrow?

You can borrow 100% of your car’s purchase value and include extras such as insurance and extended warranties.

5. What is a comparison rate?

When looking at interest rates for equipment loans, an interest rate shows you the total interest you will pay on the principal. A comparison rate includes most fees and charges expressed as a total interest rate. You can compare similar loans using a comparison rate table.

6. How long does the process take?

We can approve finance within 24 hours. If you require immediate approval, our consultants are standing by.

7. How long can you go on a equipment loan?

The average equipment loan term is 5 years. People can opt for shorter or longer terms depending on their needs and circumstances. Some equipment loans can last for 12 months all the way up to 7 years.

8. Can I still get a equipment loan if I am self-employed?
